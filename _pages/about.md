I am a first-year PhD candidate in the College of Engineering at [City University of Hong Kong](https://www.cityu.edu.hk/). My research focuses on tropical cyclone (TC) dynamics, with particular interests in intensification, eyewall replacement cycles (ERC), and climatological of TCs. 

I was fortunate to receive my master’s training under Professor [Qiaoyan Wu](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=xtOljf4AAAAJ&hl=en), and I am currently supervised by Professor [Ping Lu](https://scholars.cityu.edu.hk/en/persons/plu222/). 

I am excited to further explore the dynamics governing TCs from genesis to peak intensity, with a particular focus on rapid intensification (RI) and eyewall replacement cycles (ERC). The former is a necessary process for TCs to reach higher intensity levels, while the latter represents a complex internal reorganization experienced by most extreme TCs. Currently, my PhD research is primarily focus on ERCs.
